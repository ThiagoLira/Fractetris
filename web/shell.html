What you're seeing: one Game Boy Tetris screen — except every single pixel of it is also a complete Tetris game. All 23,040 of them play themselves in parallel on your GPU, and each one is tinted by the color of its pixel in the big game, so together they form the picture. Zoom in and the image dissolves into thousands of tiny live boards; zoom out and their brightness reassembles the screen you were just playing. Big game and micro-games alike run a faithful recreation of the original 1989 Game Boy code. A faithful recreation of the original 1989 Game Boy Tetris — gravity tables, the quirky piece randomizer, line-clear timings and all — reverse-engineered from the original assembly into C, dressed in Rio scenery.
